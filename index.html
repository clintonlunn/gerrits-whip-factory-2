About Gerrit's Whip Factory

Gerrit is a whipmaker based out of Southern California. He produces high quality bullwhips and snake whips made from 650 nylon paracord. All whips are hand made one at a time and most are custom ordered. He makes whips of all sizes but his most common are 6 and 8 foot bullwhips for adults, and 5 foot whips for children under 10 years old.

Gerrit has been cracking a whip since he was 10 years old. He received his first whip as a birthday present, which was 10 plait cowhide bullwhip. It served him well (except that one issue with the TSA) and is still in his collection. However a few years of neglect as a child left it in a state where its better suited living out its life retired and on a shelf. Thats when he turned to nylon bullwhips.

While performing in a circus he was introduced to a paracord bullwhip a coworker had made, and was impressed by the quality. After some research he decided to attempt to make one himself. The results were… poor. At the very least it was functional, but not what one would call a quality product. More paracord was ordered and he began honing his skills as he fell down the rabbit hole of whipmaking.

To this day Gerrit is still honing his skills and tweaking things. He experiments with new plaiting patterns, color combinations, and takes meticulous notes on almost every aspect of his whips so each one can be an improvement on the last. This is why every whip that leaves his workbench comes with a satisfaction guarantee. If you are ever dissatisfied with the quality of a whip you can return it for a full refund. As a perpetual student Gerrit welcomes any whipmakers or whip enthusiasts feedback on his products. Gerrit is always aiming to improve so he can create the highest quality product, otherwise he wouldn’t put his name on it.

Core

The foundation of our whips.

The core is a thin steel rod inserted into a sinker cord. This core is shot-loaded if requested and thatched to the rod using artificial sinew. The handle can be any length desired though 8-10 inches is standard with 12 inches being the rare exception. The core is then bolstered before being encased in the first belly.

Internal Bellies

Bellies aid in the structure and taper of your whip.

By default, any whip over 5’ will have 2 internal bellies. These are plaited in traditional whipmaker’s plait in the handle and thong and with a tight 1x1 checkerboard pattern in the transition. This assists in the bolstering and creates a smooth transition of the whip.

If you require a different method - no worries. We will accommodate your needs as best we can.

Overlay

The colorful exterior of your whip.

The final overlay is the only part of your whip that can be seen from the exterior. It’s the layer where we can have the most fun with color combinations. This is where your color choice, handle design, and thong pattern really shine and is the characteristic that makes nylon whips truly unique. From vibrant colors, unique patterns, earth tones, black and white, or solid colors we can accommodate almost any desire. If you have any questions, reach out to us. Chances are we’ll be able to come up with a custom design for you to meet your needs.

Since November of 2020, the thong is always braided in whipmaker’s plait in a method that avoids the dreaded “double-strand”. For any whips before this date that are still in stock, there may be a “double-strand” where a strand has been dropped. This is very common in whips and is simply a characteristic of the standard plaiting method Gerrit learned originally. To the untrained eye, it is hardly noticeable. If anyone does notice it, it is a great opportunity to bore all your friends with your knowledge on the construction of a whip. (Or is that just what Gerrit does?)

Fall and Fall-hitch

the final taper to your whip.

Whips are ended in a standard four point fall hitch that encompasses the fall. The final hitch is passed up under the other 3 hitches and the fall itself, ensuring the knot will hold tightly throughout the life of the whip. This creates the tightest ending to the thong allowing for the smoothest transition possible.

Due to the nature of tying this knot, any whips sent in for repairs where: the fall hitch has been removed, the knot untied, or other situations where the fall hitch has been altered, the whip may lose an inch or two of the overall length to retie. Of course, this is avoided at all costs and we will notify you before this happens.

The falls themselves are a single strand spliced into itself with the internal portion is half the length of the exterior. This allows for a continued taper in the fall itself ending your whip in a single nylon strand. The fall is then attached above the fall hitch and then subsequently tied on with the fall hitch itself.

The fall along with the cracker are the only two parts of a whip that will wear out over time. These can be replaced without untying the fall hitch completely and it is recommended that you never untie the fall hitch unless absolutely necessary. Please see our repairs section if you are not comfortable making these repairs yourself.

Crackers

The part that goes bang!

Crackers are made out of black kevlar thread that has been twisted together. They are all hand-made, so lengths vary, but they aim to be 6” long unless otherwise requested. The kevlar thread is very resilient, and though it will wear out eventually, has a long life. This will provide thousands of cracks for hobbyist whip crackers and professionals alike. Though, like all crackers, their life will quickly diminish when used to cut through objects.

They are attached to the fall using a cow hitch that has been passed through the center of the fall using a micro lacing needle. These can be undone without any special equipment; however, if you aim to replace the cracker and don’t have a lacing needle you will have difficulties. Contact us for recommended methods of replacing the cracker without special tools.

Heel and Transition Knots

Intricate and decorative knot-work.

These are the decorative covers for transition from handle to thong and mark the end of the whip.

The heel knot is what covers the base of your whip and adds a foundation to keep your hand from sliding off the handle. There are several knot combinations available to use here, each providing equal support. The choice between which knot you want is purely aesthetic.

The transition knot is an optional knot to add where the handle meets the thong. The goal of this knot is to hide the changes the plaiting makes during this transition. This isn’t necessary in all cases though, as it has little physical importance to the structure of the whip. Gerrit himself goes back in forth on whether or not to add this knot depending on the braiding on the handle. That said, in any whip where the handle braiding doesn’t match the thong it is highly recommended to hide this transition. When the handle colors don’t match the colors of the thong the transition knot is necessary, there’s no way to make that transition pretty and we need to hide it.

Overall Length

Length from heel to fall hitch.

Overall Length is the total length from the base of the handle to the fall hitch (the end of the braided section).

Children's whips come in a recommended size between 4 and 6 feet long. These are easier to manage for children, with 6 foot whips being an intermediate whip that suits both children and adults. All whips under 6 feet long will be made with only one internal belly which allows for a smaller handle suitable for children by default.

For adults purchasing their first whip a 6 or 8 foot whip is usually recommended. 6 foot whips are a little "quicker" and most people find them easier to control for more complicated cracking patterns. 8 foot whips are capable of doing all the cracks a 6 foot whip can but will move "slower". The extra length allows a bit more energy to be put into the whip making louder cracks easier.

Custom length whips are always an option. Feel free to contact us for more details.






Handle Length

People have their preferences, but it's really all about how you use it.

Handle length is the length of the handle from the base of the heel knot to the beginning of the thong. For a first time owner it's recommended to choose an 8 or 10 inch handle with 8 inches being a more traditional length.

Repair Services

Our repair policy, and our offers to repair other whips.

We believe in the quality of our products. If there are issues that arise outside of normal wear and tear we will happily fix the whip at no cost. Simply contact us about your issue, include photos if possible, and we will decide together what the best course of action should be.

For minor repairs such as: replacing falls, attaching crackers, or repairing scratches to the wax coating we can recommend DIY fixes. Chances are you can make these repairs yourself with a little advice and common tools. If you would rather send your whip in for repair anyway, no worries! A fall replacement and new cracker is $20 plus shipping. Fresh waxing is $25. For anything else contact us and we can arrange a price based on your needs.

We even repair whips bought from other vendors. Contact us with your issue and we will help you if we can.
